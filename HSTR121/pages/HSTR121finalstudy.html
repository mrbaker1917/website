III. Questions on Seventeen Moments in Soviet History (20 points; 4 points per year): On the final examination there will be questions about the articles on the website, Seventeen Moments in Soviet History. You will be asked to provide a brief description, 3-4 sentences, on article from five different years. The years for which articles have been assigned are 1939, 1943, 1954, 1961, 1973, 1980, 1985, 1991. Only FIVE of these will be on the final exam.

IV. Essay Question (40 points; choice of one of two): For the essay question, students will write a five-paragraph essay in response to one of two questions. It is crucial that students first of all indicate which question the are answering, then state the thesis ( answer to the question); then, make an argument for that thesis, and finally conclude. It is not sufficient to describe the events. Students must make an argument in response to the question using the readings and lecture material to support their answers. Below are sample questions that may or may not appear on the exam.
  1. What were the main causes of the Bolsheviks' victory in the civil war? Amongst these causes, which one was most important? Make an argument for this cause's crucial importance, situating it amongst the other major causes. Please be as specific as possible.
  2. "The Soviet Union's victory over Nazi Germany in WWII showed that, despite all the hardships and violence, rapid industrialization was necessary." Do you agree or disagree with this quotation? Take a side, state your thesis, and argue it using specific examples and information from the readings and class discussions. Please be as specific as possible.
  3. Did the Khrushchev period prove that the Soviet Union was not reformable or rather that it was but Khrushchev was not the right leader to do it? Take a side, state your thesis, and argue it using specific examples and information from the readings and class discussions. Please be as specific as possible.

They argue that Gorbachevâ€™s role was not decisive, but rather that he was only the accidental catalyst to collapse. Do you agree or disagree with this assessment? While comparing the various causes of the collapse of the Soviet Union, make an argument in favor or against this position. Please be as specific as possible.
