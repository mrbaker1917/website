II. Matching Questions (10 points): In this section, students will have to match various performers (from the above list) with their most famous songs.

III. Multiple-Choice Questions (20 points): Ten multiple-choice questions, each worth two points; these questions will concern material covered in the class periods and assigned readings.

IV. Essay Question (40 points; choice of one of two): For this essay question, students will write a five-paragraph essay in response to one of two questions. It is crucial that students, first of all, indicate which question they are answering, then state the thesis (answer to the question); then, make an argument for that thesis, and finally conclude. It is not sufficient to describe the events. Students must make an argument in response to the question using the readings and lecture material to support their answers. Below are sample questions that may or may not appear on the exam.
  1. What were the main reasons for the rise of Punk Rock? Amongst these causes, which one was most important? Make an argument for this cause's crucial importance, situating it amongst the other major causes. Please be as specific as possible.

  2. "Selling out is inevitable. All rock musicians, if they want to become famous or just want not to starve, will at some point sell their souls to the music industry." Do you agree or disagree with this quotation? Take a side, state your thesis, and argue it using specific historical examples and information from the readings and class discussions. Please be as specific as possible.

  3. "It's like saying, 'Get all the pop music, put it into a cartridge, put the cap on it and fire the gun,'" Pete Townshend of the Who said in 1968. "Whether those ten or 15 numbers sound roughly the same. You don't care what period they were written in, what they're all about. It's the bloody explosion that they create when you let the gun off. It's the event. That's what rock and roll is." Do you agree or disagree with this quotation? Take a side, state your thesis, and argue it using specific historical examples and information from the readings and class discussions. Please be as specific as possible.
