The Assignment:

Each student must write a critical analysis of a film about the history of Rock and Roll.

Length: 3-4 pages (Times Roman, 12 pt, double-spaced)

The analysis must answers the following questions:

  1. What was the film about (briefly)?
  2. What was the historical context in which it was produced?
  3. What do you think was the director's main goal in making this film?
  4. How does it help us to understand the history of Rock and Roll better?
  5. What problems do you see with the film, and especially the ways in which the film portrays the history of Rock and Roll?

Crucial: your critique must be analytical, critical, and not only descriptive. You should have a thesis and prove it.

Important: you do not need to use sources outside the assigned texts for the course, but you may use other scholarly sources. To find scholarly sources, begin with UVic library's website. Please feel free to ask the instructor for help in finding scholarly sources. Do not use any WEB-based sources without the instructor's permission.

You must provide a title page including the title, your name, the date, and the name of the course. If you use other sources than the film, you must include a bibliography.

Late papers will be marked down one third of a grade per day (including weekends).

Grading of the Critique:

In grading your critiques, I will above all examine whether you have addressed the assignment, answering the five questions posed above. I will also take grammar and style into account. Below is the rubric I will use to grade the assignment.
