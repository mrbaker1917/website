Course Goals:

  • To provide students with a basic understanding of the causes of the rise of Rock and Roll as a music genre.
  • To guide students through the development of the genre into a social, cultural, and economic project.
  • To encourage students to think more critically about the origins and operation of the globalized music industry.

Suggested Readings:

  • Szatmary, David P. Rockin' in Time. Boston: Pearson, 2014.
  • Marcus, Greil. History of Rock 'n' Roll in Ten Songs. New Haven: Yale University Press, 2015.
  • One article each week from Rolling Stone Magazine's freely available "Coverwall" archive [link].

Class Procedure:

Class periods will include a mixture of discussion, video presentations, and lectures. Student participation is strongly encouraged.
