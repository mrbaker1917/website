II. MAP QUESTIONS (worth 10 points; two points each; choice of 5/7):

In this section, you will be asked to identify important places on a blank map of Europe in 1914. Possible locations include the location of each of the Great Powers and their capital cities, as well as a few other places, whose location you will know if you have studied the Essential Terms listed above.

III. DOCUMENT QUESTIONS (worth 20 points; four points each, choice of 5/7):

In this section, there will be questions about the documents assigned on the course website (pre-midterm exam). Students will be provided with a fairly obvious quotation from a document, and then have to identify it by providing (1) the author, (2) the approximate date of its origin (as accurately as you can), and (3) then explain its historical significance. I will provide a short list of the documents to study for this section of the exam. Each question will be worth four points: one point for the author's name; one point for the date; and two points for a correct explanation of the document's historical significance.

III. ESSAY QUESTION (worth 60 points):

For the essay question, you will have a choice of ONE out of TWO. It is crucial that you first of all state the question, then your thesis statement (your answer to the question). Then, make an argument for your thesis, and finally conclude.  It is not sufficient to describe the events.  You must make an argument in response to the question. Below are sample questions that may or may not appear on the exam.
  1. What was the "Dual Revolution" that occurred in Europe in the nineteenth century? Discuss why these revolutions were important and what were their most significant consequences for nineteen-century developments. Please be as specific as possible.

  2. What were the major causes of the French Revolution? Amongst these, which cause do you think was most important? Why? Make an argument for your most important cause, comparing it to the other major causes. Please be as specific as possible.

  3. What were the main causes for the rise of nationalism in the Western World? Which of these causes was decisive? Make an argument for your most important cause, comparing it to the other major causes. Please be as specific as possible.

  4. Many historians have argued that the Industrial Revolution was the most important "event" in the world since the beginning of Neolithic Age. Do you agree or disagree? Discuss with reference to specific developments in the nineteenth century. Please be as specific as possible.
